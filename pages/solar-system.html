The Sun

The Sun The luminous heart of our system, a massive sphere of plasma that provides the energy for all life on Earth.
The Sun's gravity holds the solar system together, keeping everything from the biggest planets to the smallest particles of debris in its orbit.

Mercury

Mercury The smallest planet, racing through space with extreme temperature swings and a rugged, cratered surface.
Mercury has extreme temperature swings and a heavily cratered surface shaped by impacts.

Venus

Venus A world of crushing pressure, extreme heat, and a brilliant, thick atmosphere of sulfuric acid clouds.
Venus is shrouded in thick clouds and has a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet.

Earth

Earth A vibrant world of vast oceans, shifting clouds, and a complex atmosphere that sustains all known life.
Earth's atmosphere and liquid water enable life. Its magnetic field helps shield the surface from solar radiation.

Moon

Moon Earth's ancient natural satellite that governs our ocean tides and stabilizes our planet's axial tilt.
The Moon stabilizes Earth's axial tilt and drives ocean tides through gravitational interaction.

Mars

Mars A desert world of oxidized red dust, towering dormant volcanoes, and deep, ancient river valleys.
Mars shows evidence of ancient rivers and lakes. Olympus Mons is the tallest volcano in the solar system.

Jupiter

Jupiter The solar system's massive gas giant, dominated by ancient swirling storms and the iconic, centuries-old Great Red Spot.
Jupiter is the largest planet, with powerful storms and dozens of moons. Its gravity shapes the architecture of the outer solar system.

Saturn

Saturn Majestic rings composed of billions of individual ice particles and rocky debris.
Saturn's rings are made of countless ice and rock particles. The system spans hundreds of thousands of kilometers but is very thin.

Uranus

Uranus An immense ice giant that uniquely rotates on its side with a extreme axial tilt.
Uranus rotates on its side, likely due to an ancient collision. Methane in the atmosphere gives it a blue green hue.

Neptune

Neptune A turbulent world of deep blue hues, featuring the fastest supersonic winds and most violent storms in the solar system.
Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system and dramatic storms in its deep blue atmosphere.
