Local Group

Local Group A gravitational family of more than fifty galaxies, including the Milky Way, Andromeda, and Triangulum.
The Local Group spans roughly 10 million light-years and contains more than 50 galaxies, including the Milky Way, Andromeda, and Triangulum.

Canis Major Dwarf

Canis Major Dwarf A faint dwarf companion near the Milky Way, likely being stretched and absorbed by tidal forces.
Canis Major Dwarf is one of the nearest dwarf companions and is likely being disrupted as it merges into the Milky Way halo.

Magellanic Clouds

Magellanic Clouds Two irregular satellite galaxies rich in gas, actively forming stars while interacting with the Milky Way.
The Magellanic Clouds are nearby satellite galaxies rich in gas and star-forming regions, interacting gravitationally with the Milky Way.

Andromeda Galaxy

Andromeda Galaxy The nearest major spiral galaxy, moving toward us and expected to merge with the Milky Way.
Andromeda sits about 2.5 million light-years away and is expected to merge with the Milky Way in about 4 to 5 billion years.

Triangulum Galaxy

Triangulum Galaxy A smaller spiral in the Local Group, likely linked dynamically to Andromeda and nearby companions.
Triangulum is the third-largest galaxy in the Local Group and may be gravitationally linked to Andromeda as part of the same regional system.

Virgo Supercluster

Virgo Supercluster A vast concentration of galaxy groups and clusters that includes our Local Group within its structure.
The Virgo Supercluster contains tens of thousands of galaxies and includes the Local Group inside a much larger network of galaxy groups.

Cosmic Web

Cosmic Web The universe-wide network of filaments and voids where galaxies trace the underlying dark matter scaffold.
The Cosmic Web is the universe-scale pattern of filaments, clusters, and voids shaped largely by dark matter and gravity over cosmic time.

Observable Universe

Observable Universe The furthest region whose light can reach us, defining the current boundary of cosmic observation.
The Observable Universe marks the farthest distance light has reached us since the Big Bang, defining the current boundary of observation.
